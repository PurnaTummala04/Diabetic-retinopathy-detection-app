Welcome to DR Screening

Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) is an eye condition that can cause vision loss and blindness in people with diabetes. It happens when high blood sugar damages the tiny blood vessels in the retina (the light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye). Early DR may not cause symptoms, but timely screening and management can prevent serious vision problems.

Note: Many patients have no symptoms in early stages — regular screening is essential.

Important: This site provides educational support only — it is not a medical device. Always consult your doctor for diagnosis or treatment.
